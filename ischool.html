UX design case study - 2020

UofT Ischool website redesign


The coronavirus outbreak has pushed many canadian colleges to move classes to an online format. The project aims to improve the experience of educators and students when having class via video-conferencing softwares. We created an interface that connects educators and students.

My role

UX researcher, UX designer and front-end developer(reactJS)


Tools

Figma, Miro, Slack, ReactJS and Zoom

Observation


Through several participants observation sessions, we defined problems that students are currently facing in a virtual educational environment.



The inability for presenters and participants to engage and have dialogue with one another is a major issue with video conferencing. The lack of low risk, non-disruptive, simple-to-use features that allow participants to comfortably engage with presentations results in lower levels of engagement.



This lack of engagement is especially troublesome for presentations with educational objectives as engagement is a necessary component for learning.

Competitive analysis


While many software had features that engage participants, no software provided any features for participants to react and engage in a presentation without garnering overwhelming attention and/or being disrupting, while still being easily visible to the presenter.



Via our proposed system, presenters will be able to interpret participant engagement with ease through our proposed real time visualization and aggregation of participantâ€™s reactions and responses This eliminates the need for presenters to switch to different tabs or change the view mode to engage more comfortably with participants.

Mental Model


Based on findings from participants observation, we defined the mental model for "having an online class on video conferencing tools" to select groups of people whom we want to focus on to provide a tailored experience to users.



We listed distinguishing tasks and grouped them where there is behavior affinity. At this stage, we defined actors, named the groups (actors) and wrote an description.

Persona


Based on the task-based audience segments, we defined three personas.



In order to keep it manageable within the allotted time frame, we selected core groups to focus for this project.



For presenters, we decided to target those with low and medium prior knowledge of video-conferencing tools , as well as those who look forhigh participation from their audience. For participants, we targeted both shy and active participants that are focused on the presentation.

Develop

Design Evolution and High-fidelity Prototypes



The design has undergone some revision in light of the feedback we had received from the usability testing with our paper prototype. Therefore, based on findings from the usability testing with our paper prototypes, we created the high-fidelity prototypes with Figma.


There are several design evolution from low-fi prototypes: 1) clear labelling on each section, 2) "clear the queue/result" button (short-cut for the interaction), 3) improved visualization of the "level of understanding graph" and 4) constraints on the self-reported understanding bar.






Iterative usability testing


We conducted additional usability testing with the hi-fi prototype that users can interact with all functionalities. This round of usability testing consists of tasks, pre and post-test questionnaire on participants' backgrounds and subjective feedback on the system.


Even though we refined our design iteratively, we still received feedback on the complicated graph, cognitive workload and small font size.


Based on the formative feedback we have received, we made several main modifications to our design: 1) bigger font, 2) graph labels, and 3) simplified graphs.




Alpha system


Then we turned them into the working system. We developed the whole system with ReactJS and WebSocket. For our alpha system, You can check it out here!



In addition to design evolutions, we made based on the testing result, we added alt text and tooltips for the reaction emojis, considering the accessibility. Moreover, the confirmation flows on the "clear all" button were implemented in terms of error prevention.






Reflections

Always prioritize users first



Throughout this project, I've learnt that UX designers should be able to prioritize user needs. UXers are the ones who represent users in the entire project. It was easy to forget what users really wanted and needed during the process, and sometimes they are not even in the conversation. (i.e. while discussing the feasibility of each function) However, whenever I encountered the situation, I've tried to put users' needs at the top of the list by referring to the personas and mental model we defined.



The usability of some components are not easilly assessed via testings



The usability testing and evaluation methods are mandatory steps to minimize the gap between users' mental models and conceptual models of systems and increase the overall usability. However, it was not easy to gauge the effectiveness and ease of use of the graphs during the study. As this kind of information visualization comes with interactive and dynamic graphics, I was able to assess its usability with the alpha system (working prototype), in the late design phase. The graphs' design was done based on the academic literature; however, there are not sufficient numbers of usability studies focusing on data visualization. In the future study, I would like to conduct testings to figure out users' preferences on the graphs given the context (e.g. A/B testing).
