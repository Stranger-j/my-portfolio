UX Research case study - 2020

Usability testing with Steam Website


Despite that Steam has the highest number of active users and has been leading the game industry, its website is poorly designed. Users often feel disorientated when finding information on the website, since the information on the website is not properly organized, and too many visuals are randomly displayed. Therefore, by conducting usability testings, our team decided to evaluate the website's usability and find out better ways to organize information and provide a more intuitive and tailored experience to users.

Target user group


This usability testing target population is the younger generation who are currently between 18-39 years old and own a personal computer to enjoy gaming. According to the sales, demographic and usage data of 2018 from the Entertainment Software Association, the average gamer is 34 years old. Based on the same source of data, the main device to play a game is a personal computer (41%), followed by smartphone (36%) and dedicated game console (36%). Based on the target user group, I built two personas for this project.
