Foriegn language learners and subtitled videos

The study originally consisted of a laboratory experiment in which non-native speakers watch subtitled videos with the playback speed dynamically adjusted based on eye movement data. Under the supervision of Prof. Karyn Moffatt, I designed the whole project and completed a pilot study with web-based eye-tracking software.

However, due to the pandemic that seems never-ending, we pivoted this research to the qualitative research that includes user behavior observation and semi-structured interviews.

Date: From October 2019 to Present
Research Approach: Qualitative & quantitative research
Research Area: Human-computer interaction

Usability evaluation of Canadian open data portals

The study consists of a comprehensive literature review on the usability of open data portals, the analysis of current portals and interviews with experts in the field.

I conducted the literature review on the usability of the open data portal and designed usability goals, matrics and interview questions.

Date: From December 2019 to March 2020
Research Approach: Qualitative Research
Research Area: Usability evaluation and Open data portals

The public perception of bias in AI and their concern with bias and AI

The study was conducted as a mini research project of "research design and principles" course. Due to the pandemic, the data analysis is based solely on the quantitative portion of the study, using the questionnaire as a research instrument. During the project, I designed survey questions and conducted the data analysis and visualization with Python.

Date: From Jan 2020 to April 2020
Research Approach: Quantitative research
Research Area: Social science
