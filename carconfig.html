UX & Information Architecture study - 2018

Car Configurator - Hyundai.com Website


Hyundai Motors Europe needed an advanced car configurator function that considers all of the different European regulations. Moreover, despite differing requirements of local marketers, only one type of car configurator had to be launched to provide unified brand images across Europe.

My role

Business analyst and Information architecture


Tools

Jira, Codebeamer and MS office suite


Competitive analysis and Web analytics


I conducted a competitive analysis to research current and potential competitors' web configuration. Using google analytics,
I also analyzed the pains of current Hyundai's web configurator. (i.e. which page has the highest bounce rate and path analysis)



Requirement gathering


Then, we held several workshops in Germany, Spain and Korea to collect business requirements from users and stakeholders and visited the field to understand European users and business flow.



Having conducted workshops by interfacing with project stakeholders, we were able to build the Business Requirement Document (BRD) that is approved by all stakeholders.



Wireframes


I transformed requirements into flow chart first, considering legacy systems and the business flow of European offices. Then we held another meeting to get the design concept right. During the meeting, we bounced off our ideas with sketches on the whiteboard.



After finalizing the flow chart and some design ideas, I visualized all requirements with wireframes and conducted a walkthrough session with stakeholders.




Implementation!



Due to the GDPR, the implementation of this feature in Europe was postponed. However, we implemented this feature in a global template website, which is used for the global roll-out, in order to maintain consistent brand identity and customer experience.
